Understanding HIV & AIDS

Everything you need to know about the virus, the progression, and the path to safety.

What is HIV?

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) is a virus that attacks the body’s defense system—specifically CD4 cells (T cells). Without treatment, HIV turns these cells into virus factories, weakening your immunity over time.

Global Strains: While HIV-1 is the most common worldwide, our clinic screens for all strains to ensure no diagnosis is missed.

What is AIDS?

AIDS (Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome) is the most advanced stage of HIV. It is diagnosed when CD4 counts drop below 200 cells/mm³ or when opportunistic infections occur.

"AIDS is a diagnosis of the past; with modern ART, your immune system can recover and thrive even after an AIDS diagnosis."

The Testing Window

You cannot "feel" HIV. Initial flu-like symptoms disappear quickly. The only way to know is a test.

  • 2 Weeks: Antigen tests can detect viral particles.
  • 1-3 Months: Antibody tests become reliable (The Window Period).

How is HIV Spread?

Direct Risk Unprotected sex, shared needles, mother-to-child.
Zero Risk Sweat, tears, saliva, hugging, or sharing toilets.

U=U Reality: If your viral load is undetectable for 6 months, you cannot sexually transmit the virus. Treatment is the ultimate prevention.

Should You Get Tested?

Have you had sex without a condom with a partner whose status you don't know?

Are you pregnant or considering becoming pregnant?

Have you ever shared needles for any type of injection?

Humans may discriminate, but the virus does not. Testing is for everyone.
